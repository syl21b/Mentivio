What to Do in a Crisis

Recognizing the signs and knowing how to respond can make a critical difference in a mental health emergency

Recognize the Signs

Extreme mood swings, talking about suicide, severe anxiety, inability to perform daily tasks, or expressing hopelessness.

Stay Calm

Take deep breaths and try to remain calm. Your calm presence can help the person in crisis feel more secure.

Listen Without Judgment

Let the person express their feelings without interrupting or offering unsolicited advice.

Remove Immediate Dangers

If possible, remove any items that could be used for self-harm from the immediate environment.

Don't Leave Them Alone

Stay with the person until professional help arrives or the crisis passes.

Follow Up

After the immediate crisis, check in regularly and encourage ongoing professional support.

Your Mental Health Matters

Reaching out for help is a sign of strength, not weakness. Whether you're in crisis or just need someone to talk to, support is available.
