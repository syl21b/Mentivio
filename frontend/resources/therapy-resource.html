Types of Therapy

Understanding different therapeutic approaches and finding what works best for you

What is Psychotherapy?

Psychotherapy, often called "talk therapy," is when you work with a trained therapist in a safe, confidential environment to explore your feelings, thoughts, and behaviors while learning new coping skills.

How Therapy Works

During therapy sessions, you'll discuss past or current problems, experiences, thoughts, feelings, or relationships. Your therapist helps you make connections and gain insights that can lead to positive changes in your life.

Research shows psychotherapy is effective for improving symptoms across many mental health conditions. Many people find the best results come from combining therapy with medication when appropriate.

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

CBT focuses on the connections between your thoughts, feelings, and behaviors. It helps you identify and change negative thinking patterns that contribute to emotional distress.

How It Works

  • Identifies unhealthy thought patterns
  • Challenges negative beliefs
  • Develops constructive thinking habits
  • Often includes homework between sessions
Best for: Depression, Anxiety, PTSD
Duration: 12-20 sessions
Strong research support

What to Expect

  • Structured sessions with clear goals
  • Practical skills you can use daily
  • Focus on current problems and solutions
  • Active collaboration with your therapist

CBT has been shown to create positive changes in brain activity, improving overall functioning.

Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT)

DBT combines CBT techniques with mindfulness practices, focusing on accepting difficult feelings while working to change unhealthy behaviors.

Core Components

  • Mindfulness and emotional regulation
  • Distress tolerance skills
  • Interpersonal effectiveness
  • Balance of acceptance and change
Best for: BPD, Self-harm, Suicidal thoughts
Duration: Longer-term
Often includes group therapy

Unique Features

  • Emphasis on validation and acceptance
  • Skills training for real-life situations
  • Phone coaching between sessions
  • Therapist consultation team support

Psychodynamic Therapy

This approach explores how past experiences and unconscious thoughts influence your current behavior and relationships.

Approach

  • Explores unconscious patterns
  • Examines childhood experiences
  • Focuses on relationship patterns
  • Uses free association and dream analysis
Best for: Deep-seated patterns, Personality disorders
Duration: Often longer-term

Other Therapy Approaches

There are many effective therapy approaches. The best one depends on your individual needs and preferences.

EMDR Therapy

Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing helps process traumatic memories through bilateral stimulation.

Best for: PTSD, Trauma

Interpersonal Therapy

Focuses on improving relationships and social functioning to relieve symptoms.

Best for: Depression, Relationship issues
Duration: 12-16 weeks

Animal-Assisted Therapy

Uses trained therapy animals to reduce anxiety, depression, and provide comfort.

Best for: Anxiety, PTSD, Hospital settings

Mindfulness Therapies

Combines meditation and awareness practices with traditional therapy techniques.

Best for: Stress, Anxiety, Depression prevention

How to Choose the Right Therapy

Finding the right therapy approach and therapist is key to successful treatment. Consider these factors:

Your Goals

What do you want to achieve? Symptom relief? Personal growth? Better relationships? Different therapies focus on different outcomes.

Evidence Base

Look for therapies with research support for your specific concerns. CBT and DBT have strong evidence for many conditions.

Therapist Fit

The relationship with your therapist is crucial. You should feel comfortable, understood, and respected.

Practical Factors

Consider cost, location, availability, insurance coverage, and time commitment when making your decision.

Remember: It's okay to try different therapists or approaches until you find what works best for you. Good therapy should feel like a collaborative partnership.
